Specific REI Partnership Angles
REI Co-op Classes
Beginner kayaking, backpacking basics, rock climbing intro — kindred QR cards handed out at every class registration. "Loved the class? Find your crew for the next trip."
REI Adventures
Fills solo spots in group guided trips. Solo travelers join a guided trip with strangers — same as kindred's model. kindred = the free version. REI Adventures = the premium version. They're compatible, not competitive.
Gear Discount for Trip Leaders
kindred trip leaders (Evan archetype) get 15% off at REI as a leader perk. REI gets acquisition of their highest-value customer: someone who regularly organizes outdoor trips and influences 10+ other purchases.
In-Store QR + "First Timers" Campaign
QR codes in beginner gear sections, at checkout, at Classes signup kiosks. Co-branded "First Timers" content series: REI gear + kindred crew = your first adventure.
Why REI Says Yes

REI's biggest problem isn't selling gear — it's that people buy gear and stop using it. A $300 tent that sits in a closet is a failed conversion. kindred solves the utilization problem: people who have gear but no crew to go with. When someone finds their adventure crew on kindred, they buy more gear. REI's mission AND their revenue depend on people actually going outside.

Partnership Model

Rafting companies have one persistent problem: filling the last 1–2 spots on each raft. A 6-person raft with 4 booked slots loses margin and sometimes cancels trips. kindred provides a solution: "Meet your raft crew before the trip." Physical flyers at check-in, QR codes on the booking confirmation email, or a simple integration where solo bookings get introduced to each other on kindred before they show up at the put-in. After the trip, kindred keeps the crew together for the next river.
